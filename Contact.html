The Future is Now!

3D printing is here. It's now. It's disruptive. And we've only just begun to fathom its potential.

Today 3D printing is like the PC was in the early 80's – they were out there, a niche market, a few people dabbled with them and early adopters saw the potential – but for the consumer market, people couldn't afford them, couldn't work or operate them or foresee how the PC was going to change the world. Roughly 30 years later, history is rhyming with itself with 3D printing.

3D printing will change humanity in ways we can barely conceive at this moment in time. It will be a growing part of the 21st Century economy. 3D printing will have impacts on both the macro and micro-economic scales; its has the capability to give companies a competitive edge as well as become a platform for open-source design. It will be a technology that gives access to necessities people need. It is a technology that will enhance the critical thinking and design skills of all ages.

A 3D printer is an amazing little box – so much goes into it and so much can come from it. Consider all the science,technology and skills that go into the 3D design and printing process – you are combining engineering, math, design, electronics, materials, chemistry, heat transfer, fluid flow and computer programming – in one little box; and with a computer, you can become your own elector-mechanical-programming design engineer – at any time and at any age. And with today's children, we will have a generation who will grow up with this technology, with great imagination and fresh ideas and are going to be the creators and designers of tomorrow.

The 3D printer is a box where minds and technologist all mix together to come up with seemingly unlimited possibilities.
